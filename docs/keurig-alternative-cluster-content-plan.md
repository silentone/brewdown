### Section 4: Method deep dives (core comparison)

**H2:** The best Keurig alternatives, ranked by what matters

Use H3 per method. Repeat a consistent mini-template for each.

**Mini-template per method:**
1. One-sentence verdict (bold callout).
2. Cost snapshot (ingredient $/cup + typical machine range from calculator defaults).
3. Brew time and active attention required.
4. Cleanup reality (30 seconds honest).
5. Taste profile in plain language (body, clarity, strength) without cupping jargon.
6. Who it's for / who should skip.

**H3 prompts:**

**4a. Drip coffee maker**
- Verdict prompt: Best balance of set-and-forget convenience and low per-cup cost for multi-cup households.
- Defaults: $120 machine, $15/lb beans, 10 g/cup → ~$0.33 ingredient per cup.
- Note: can brew a pot; good when two or more people drink coffee.

**4b. French press**
- Verdict prompt: Cheapest path to noticeably better taste than pods with minimal gear.
- Defaults: $30 machine, 12 g/cup → ~$0.40 ingredient per cup.
- Address grit and cleanup objections in two sentences.

**4c. Pour-over**
- Verdict prompt: Best flavor clarity for people who do not mind two to three minutes of hands-on brewing.
- Defaults: $40 machine (dripper + kettle allowance), 11 g/cup → ~$0.36 ingredient per cup.

**4d. AeroPress**
- Verdict prompt: Best travel and small-kitchen option; fast, consistent, easy to learn.
- Note: not a calculator preset; use illustrative $30 to $40 gear and same $15/lb, ~11 to 12 g/cup as pour-over for cost ballpark (~$0.36 to $0.40/cup).

**4e. Bean-to-cup (super-automatic)**
- Verdict prompt: True one-touch upgrade if you will pay higher upfront machine cost for long-run savings vs pods.
- Defaults: $800 machine, 14 g/cup → ~$0.46 ingredient per cup; emphasize cumulative chart crossover vs pods over time (calculator CTA).

**4f. K-Cup / Keurig (baseline, not a recommendation)**
- Verdict prompt: Convenient baseline, highest ingredient cost in the cluster.
- Defaults: $0.80/pod, $120 machine. Use as comparison anchor only.

**Word target:** 900 to 1,100 words across all H3s.

### Section 3: Methodology

**H2:** How we calculate cost per cup

**Section prompt:**
- **Bulk methods formula:**  
  `ingredient cost per cup = grams per cup × (cost per lb ÷ 453)`
- **Pods formula:**  
  `ingredient cost per cup = cost per pod × pods per cup`
- Work one example longhand (French press):  
  `12 × ($15 ÷ 453) = $0.397` → round to **$0.40** in prose.
- Clarify what this section does **not** include: machine upfront, café spend, filters/consumables (pour-over filters optional; note `annualConsumables` default 0).
- One paragraph aligning with About page: machine cost appears on day one in cumulative comparisons; this post isolates ingredient line for apples-to-apples pod vs bulk comparison.
- Link to `/about` for full model.

**Word target:** 250 to 350 words.

### Section 3: Before day 1 (15-minute prep)

**H2:** Before you start: grind, storage, and expectations

**Section prompt:**
- **Grind:** coarse for French press; if using store grind, ask for French press/coarse.
- **Storage:** airtight, cool, dark; buy one to two weeks of beans at a time.
- **Ratio starting point:** 1:15 coffee to water by weight if they have a scale; otherwise 2 tbsp per 6 oz cup as a loose starting point (plain language).
- **Expectation:** first cup may be weaker or stronger than pods; adjustment is normal.
- One sentence on caffeine: similar if you use similar coffee mass.

**Word target:** 200 to 280 words.

### Section 4: Day-by-day plan

**H2:** Your 7-day plan to quit Keurig pods

Each day gets an H3 with: **Goal**, **Steps**, **Success check**, **If it goes wrong**.

**Day 1 — H3: Buy beans and brew your first French press**
- Goal: One drinkable cup without a pod machine.
- Steps: shop or order Tier 1; boil water, rest 30 seconds; 4-minute steep; plunge slowly.
- Success check: Finished a cup; noted too weak/too strong/bitter.
- If wrong: adjust coffee amount tomorrow, not brew time yet.

**Day 2 — H3: Repeat and dial in strength**
- Goal: Match or beat yesterday's taste.
- Steps: change only one variable (more or less coffee by one scoop).
- Success check: Can repeat the recipe without notes.
- If wrong: bitter → slightly coarser grind or shorter steep; weak → more coffee.

**Day 3 — H3: Cleanup routine under two minutes**
- Goal: Make cleanup feel smaller than pod guilt about waste.
- Steps: rinse press immediately; disassemble plunger; air dry.
- Success check: Sink clear in under 2 minutes.
- If wrong: keep a rinse tub in sink; still faster than pod landfill for many households (no lecturing).

**Day 4 — H3: Busy morning drill (8 minutes or less)**
- Goal: Time the full routine once.
- Steps: boil water while dressing; parallelize; no phone scrolling.
- Success check: Cup in hand within 10 minutes of waking.
- If wrong: boil extra water night before in insulated bottle (optional hack).

**Day 5 — H3: Add AeroPress as backup**
- Goal: Second method for travel or rush days.
- Steps: buy or unbox AeroPress; follow inverted or standard recipe on card; 11 g coffee illustrative.
- Success check: One acceptable cup without French press.
- Link Post 1 AeroPress blurb for why this is the backup.

**Day 6 — H3: Buy one week of beans only**
- Goal: Right-size purchase; avoid stale bag guilt.
- Steps: note consumption from week one; buy 1 to 2 weeks supply.
- Success check: No pod purchase this week.
- If wrong: keep a 6-pack of pods for guests only if household agrees (harm reduction, not failure).

**Day 7 — H3: Benchmark savings and lock your routine**
- Goal: Quantify habit change and choose primary + backup method.
- Steps: count cups brewed at home; open calculator with pods vs French press (or your path); enter actual pod price paid and beans price.
- Success check: Saved comparison screenshot or noted monthly ingredient delta.
- CTA: calculator link; Post 2 for assumption details.

**Word target:** 900 to 1,100 words across all days.
